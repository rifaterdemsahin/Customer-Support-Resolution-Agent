⚡ Challenges We Learned From

Building the animated demo was not a straight line. Every iteration revealed something unexpected. The biggest surprise: voice-over timing reshaped the entire narrative structure.

🎙️ Voice-Over: Text Is Longer Than You Think

When recording narration, we discovered that text expands dramatically when spoken. A sentence that reads in 3 seconds silently takes 6–8 seconds spoken at natural pace. This forced us to rethink the entire timeline.

  • Written estimates fail. You must record, listen, and measure real audio duration — never guess from word count.
  • Subtitles compound the problem. Each subtitle phrase needs reveal time, hold time, and fade-out — all stacking onto the timeline.
  • Iteration cycle: Write → Record → Time → Adjust script → Re-record → Re-time. Three cycles minimum per act.
"I thought Act 1 would be 4 seconds. The narration alone was 8."

⏱️ The 25/50/25 Rule Emerged From Failure

Our initial assumption was equal-length acts — 10s each for a clean 30s demo. Reality forced a different split:

The core of the message. Policy check, decision, escalation, success — the chain takes time.

Brief and memorable. Side-by-side comparison and the final takeaway — no more needed.

Act 2 carries the weight because the solution needs to land. The problem is simple to state. The lesson is simple to remember. But the transition from naive to resilient — the detection, the decision, the escalation — that's where the learning happens. It deserves half the runtime.

🔄 Iterate, Don't Perfect

Every cycle of recording and timing revealed new micro-adjustments. Subtitle reveals that overlapped awkwardly. Audio that outpaced the visuals. Scene transitions that needed an extra 0.3s of breathing room.

  • Debug panel was born from this. We needed real-time feedback on scene timing, subtitle sync, and event ordering — so we built it into every act file.
  • Act files went self-contained. Originally a single 1474-line combined.html. Split into independent acts so each could be iterated, timed, and debugged in isolation.
  • The 10-minute health check. Automated link scanning, sentinel verification, and error catching — because manual checks after every iteration don't scale.

What We Carry Forward

The gap between what you think the timing should be and what the audio actually demands is the gap where real learning happens. Voice-over narration is not a script — it's a constraint. The visuals serve the audio, not the other way around. When the text is longer than expected, you don't rush the voice — you restructure the timeline. The 25/50/25 split isn't a design choice. It's the shape the content demanded.
